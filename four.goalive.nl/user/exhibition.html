Reharvest


I designed an exhibition for Stichting Innovatie Glastuinbouw Nederland (SIGN), from idea to concept and final production.

The exhibition shows the various material flows emerging from horticulture, and how different case studies reapply these materials as valuable resources across other industries.

One of the design challenges was to create a modular structure to fit various exhibition spaces and be appealing to diverse audiences.

Positioned as a beating heart, an abstracted glasshouse conveys its role as the life behind the materials. This object contains live greenhouse plants, with lights and an irrigation system.

Four frames with long, printed fabrics featuring text and images emerge from this centre. Items like plant materials and juices are exhibited on the flat surfaces of the fabric. Each frame tells the story of a specific resource category:

  • Dry plant fibres
  • Plant juices
  • Energy production
  • Food and flowers
Reharvest is designed as a modular system that can be adapted to different exhibition spaces. The structure can be reconfigured into four (or more) layouts, making it flexible for museums, events, hallways, or entrance halls. It is lightweight, easy to transport, and quick to assemble and disassemble.
